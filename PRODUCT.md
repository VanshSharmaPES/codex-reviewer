# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individual developers, engineering teams, and repository maintainers use Codex Reviewer while reviewing code, investigating pull-request findings, and monitoring convention health across repositories.

## Product Purpose

Codex Reviewer is a repository-aware code review tool that combines structural analysis, learned conventions, and explainable AI findings. Its interface should help users understand what changed, why a finding matters, what evidence supports it, and whether a suggested fix is safe.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, unobtrusive. The product should feel like a genuine daily engineering tool: calm, clear, and useful under pressure rather than theatrical or promotional.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI dashboards, decorative gradients, excessive neon, dense walls of cards, vague AI language, unexplained confidence scores, and interfaces that make a review feel like a demo instead of an engineering workflow.

## Design Principles

- Make evidence and changed code easier to understand than the model behind them.
- Prefer useful density and strong hierarchy over decorative surfaces.
- Treat every result as a state with clear provenance: pending, passed, failed, partial, or unavailable.
- Keep actions explicit and reversible, especially for generated fixes and pull requests.
- Practice the product’s promise of convention and consistency through its own interface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard access. Never use color as the only status signal; pair state colors with text or icons. Respect reduced-motion preferences, provide visible focus states, and keep review content usable at narrow widths and enlarged text sizes.
